You do not need a specific booking slot, simply follow the below arrival instructions:

Compulsory Pre-Arrival halls Induction

Please complete your Pre-Arrival halls Induction which can be found in your halls portal and selecting the ‘Your halls induction 2023 24’ tab. Now follow the simple process and carefully read through each page before accepting. The induction has been designed to help you get ready for life in halls of residence. You should aim to spend at least 5 minutes reading each page.

This compulsory process needs to be completed by all incoming halls students before arrival.

What will my postal address be and where are my halls?

Your postal address will be your halls/block name and room number (found in your room offer within the halls Portal) followed by the relevant halls address.

View halls of residence addresses and maps.

Why do I need to collect my student card on arrival?

Most of our halls of residence are accessed via this student card (but we also have back-up door codes available if needed). Your ID is also used to access other services along with getting discounted bus access.

Is cleaning provided in rooms and communal areas?

No, a cleaning service will be provided in the stairwells, However, it is the student’s responsibility to keep rooms, kitchens, communal areas and corridors and communal toilets (where provided) clean along with transporting rubbish and waste to the relevant bins.

Is bedding included and what size is my bed?

Bedding is not provided. All Hardwick, Challinor, Park Villas, Oxstalls & Ermin Hall rooms feature a standard-size single bed.

All Pittville rooms* feature a 3/4 size bed (also known as a small double.) These beds are 120cm X 190cm/ 4′ x 6’3″ in size. *Pittville Studio rooms feature a standard size double bed.

What support is offered to halls students?

The residential support team (see above) are responsible for pastoral support to residential students living in halls. Our Residential Support Advisers (RSAs) are on hand to assist you with a variety of issues that may arise while living in halls of residence, including welfare.

Consisting of three members of staff, RSAs offer pastoral care and welfare advice to students in Halls of Residence. They help students settle in and provide support throughout the year. Another part of the role is to ensure all students abide by the Halls of Residence terms and conditions.

Is it possible also to stay in Halls again during the second year?

Yes, students can live in halls for each of their academic years. Information regarding returning to Halls for the next academic year will be sent to halls students in November 2023.

Can I extend my stay in Halls after my 2023/24 Halls contract ends in June 2024?*

Short extensions will only be agreed if the course requires it. However, there will be halls options available during the summer period for students who want to remain, but this would involve moving to a different halls complex. An email will be sent to all halls students regarding summer accommodation options in April 2024. 

*This is not relevant to the Allied Health students who are contracted from January to December 2024.

I have a car; can I bring it with me if I live in Halls?

Unfortunately, students living within halls of residence are not allowed a car in Cheltenham or Gloucester. The only exception is normally for students with disabilities or medical conditions. Permission is only given once a letter from a medical professional is provided specifically stating the need for a motor vehicle due to a medical condition/disability.

Do I need a TV licence if I live in Halls?

Yes, if you have a TV in your room or watch content on any other device. View TV licensing information.

Is there a bus service that runs between campuses?

Yes, the N bus service runs regularly during the day between Cheltenham University Campuses (including Pittville Student Village.)

The 94 bus service runs between Cheltenham town centre to Oxstalls Campus and onto Gloucester city centre.

It currently costs £2 per journey.
